The Special Stuff
The global lottery phenomenon has shown without doubt that people are incapable of picking random numbers as well as a computer.

Picker is program that uses the sort of mechanical logic used in a real lottery machine to pick numbers. Just like a real lottery machine, picker does not attempt to predict but simply bounces balls about until one hits the open trapdoor and gets selected. Unlike other programs that magic up a set of numbers, with picker you get to see the balls flying about.

With the picker you can select the balls you want in play and how many of them you want the picker to pick. These settings are preserved for each session. Picker now allows you to set the release angle for the balls.

A separate analysis module is provided for registered users. This allows you to identify trends in balls drawn to date and use them to influence the choice of balls you want in play. I plan to do a lot more on the analysis module.

Y-Knot indeed

Most lottery programs I've tried are too clinical, most are for the hardened lottery player. I wanted to write something that challenged my programming skills, something that picked numbers in the same way as a real lottery machine.

Picker and the analysis module are just the first two parts of the picker lottery suite. In the near future I hope to be developing a number of other really cool programs on the lottery theme.

Also you may ask why price it at only 5 US dollars, how is it ever going to make me any money?

The simple answer is that it wont at that price unless I sell a heck of a lot of registrations. Frankly it doesnt bother me. I've allready more than recouped the cost of Delphi, the only cost that went into making picker, except my time. For me, this is a hobby, I actually have a good job with a major IT consultancy and have the sort of skills that will always be in demand.

Producing programs like picker are a way of challenging myself and testing out ideas. The money isn't that important otherwise why would I offer to give picker away for free to anyone who gives me an idea.
